Computational Engineering

Computational Engineering

  • Computer Science or Computer Engineering - use some domains in the former and can solve some problems in the latter
  • Generally, it's the intersection of computer science and engineering
  • Utilising computer power to solve complex engineering problems
  • More specifically, it employs computational models and virtual design for engineering tasks in a simulation-driven approach

Computational Engineering (cont.)

  • Often involves high-performance computing (HPC), parallel computing, algorithms & data structure, numerical methods, modelling & simulation, data visualisation, etc.
  • Includes CAD, CAE, FEA, CFD, DSO, DoE
  • Widely applied to Aerospace Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Nuclear Engineering, Civil Engineering, Environmental Engineering, Material Science, Physics, and so on

Tile Feedhole Configuration

  • Tiles with TBC can only withstand a certain temperature range
  • Feedholes are on the liner while pedestals, studs, and ports on tile
  • Feedhole configuration impacts mass flow consumption, thereby affecting fuel consumption and the lifespan of the tiles

  • Respect constraints:
    Geometry constraints: stress analysis
    Thermal constraints: different regions on the tile have different temperature limits

Pedestal Tile Optimisation Platform

  • Rolls-Royce 1st parallelised automatic tile optimisation tool
  • Works in 3 modes:
    local design + local solve, local design+remote solve, fully remote
  • Surrogate model based approach for optimisation

Tile Optimisation Package Application

  • Achieved over 17% reduction in mass flow on 2 tile models
  • Another model exhibited a reduction in mass flow of over 35%
  • This improvement holds the potential to save millions of pounds annually in fuel costs, considering the number of Rolls-Royce engines in operation
  • All constraints have been respected; maximum tile temperature could be decreased by approximately 7°C that improves tile lifespan
  • Rig test validation of results conducted at Loughborough University
  • Applied to 8 sets of tile design and optimisation for an engine commissioned by Airbus

Engineering Challenges

  • General-purpose AI has shown effectiveness, but the application in engineering demands millimeter-level model accuracy
  • Challenges in collecting extensive CFD/FEA simulation data hinder progress in engineering AI (e.g. $3 \times 18TB$ hard disks ~ $1,000$ CFD runs)
  • Dealing with 3D volumetric data entails time-consuming tasks, e.g. some CFD requires months to solve
  • Expensive hardware requirements (servers, cluster nodes, GPU cards, external hard disks), e.g. 2 servers, each equipped with 1,024GB RAM and 4 x NVIDIA A100 80GB GPU cards ($£20,000$ per GPU card)

Design 3D using 2D Knowledge

  • ML techniques such as NN, CNN, GAN are good at understanding 2D images
  • CFD flow field depicts important information about the engine performance
  • 2D reduces the size of the data for processing, hence find solutions faster
