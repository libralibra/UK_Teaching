Overview

A Methodology for requirements analysis in AI architecture authoring tools


  • Understanding the Methodology: in-depth comprehension of the paper
  • Critical Analysis: critical thinking and discussions about the strengths, weaknesses, and practical applicability of the proposed methodology
  • Application and Relevance: how the methodology aligns with contemporary practices in AI architecture design and how it addresses existing challenges
  • Engaging Discussions: interactive session to share insights, pose questions, and engage in meaningful discussions about the paper's contributions
  • Knowledge Exchange: exchange of ideas, experiences, and perspectives

Main Contributions

  • SSS requirements analysis
  • Process mapping techniques
  • Case study approach

SSS Component Analysis

  • Proposed a process to identify and improve the authoring challenges of different interactive virtual agent (IVA) architectures
  • Used SSS to capture architecture-specific challenges and opportunities for IVA authoring process
  • They found that SSS Components are the common patterns of authoring difficulty within each SSS

Process Mapping Techniques

  • A tool for creating a visual representation of the authoring procedure
  • Making explicit steps, sub-steps, durations, bottlenecks, and dependencies involved
  • Helped to validate the SSS components and authoring support strategies

Case Study Approach

  • Applied SSS components to three case studies of different IVA architectures
    - BOD/POSH
    - FAtiMA
    - ABL
  • Used a simple scenario of "Lost Interpreter" to compare the authoring processes and outcomes of each architecture
  • Extracted the SSS and the SSS Components of each architecture
    - similarities, differences, and trade-offs between architectures
  • Proposed authoring support strategies to reduce the authoring burden

Discussion of SSS and Its Implications

  • Discussed the benefits and limitations of their methodology
  • Clarified common authoring challenges and strategies that emerged from the case studies
  • Suggested how the SSS methodology can be used to compare and evaluate different IVA architectures and their authoring affordances

The Methodology

Key Components

  • Requirement Elicitation Techniques
    - a diverse set of such techniques, including interviews, survey, and case studies
  • Domain Analysis and Knowledge Gathering
    - To understand the context, domain-specific requirements, constraints, and challenges
    - examining existing systems, standards, and best practices in the IVA domain
  • Use Case Scenarios and Prototyping
    - create use case scenarios to capture user interactions and system behaviours
    - prototyping plays a crucial role in visualising and validating requirements

The System Specific Step (SSS)

Applying SSS Methodology

  • Conducted interview with 5 institutions, survey 6+ local and remote ABL authors
  • Intermediate-expert authors paired with an analyst who is no an expert in the architecture
  • Translate the work into Process map
  • Create a visual representation of the procedure similar to flowcharts
  • Make as mush as the authoring process as explicit as possible for analysis

The Scenario

  • Lost Interpreter: armed soldiers searching for their lost interpreter via a photograph (A simplified version of IMMERSE group in ABL)
  • Why this scenario?
    • Exercises a wide range of capabilities of interactive characters:
      - player
      - multiple NPCs with different attitudes
      - physical objects
      - communication between NPCs and PC
      - multiple outcomes
    • Simple enough to reach a pseudocode state in a reasonable amount of time (1-3 hours)
      - get rid of IMMERSE's original non-verbal communication via gesture recognition

The Case Studies

  • Three teams with 1, 2, and 5 interview participants
  • Each team with a different design philosophy
  • Provides a description of the authoring process associated with the architecture
  • Creates a list of the SSS components abstracted from the authoring description
  • Includes a description of authoring support strategies that could reduce the authoring burden of the particular SSS.
  • Problem found? - no standardised method for testing and debugging

Case Study 1: BOD using POSH

  • BOD: Behaviour-Oriented Design that combined Object-Oriented Design and Behaviour-Based AI
  • POSH: Parallel-rooted, Ordered Slip-stack Hierarchical planner - Why?
    • simplicity and iteration
    • low barrier to entry for novice authors
    • encoded in the least amount of time
  • Unique case? - to have an explicit authoring approach as well as the graphical design tool (ABODE)

Design Philosophy of BOD

  • BOD designer never needs to encounter anything more complicated than graphical interface
  • Separation of the two roles: designer and programmer to be the most independent

BOD/POSH

Behaviour-Oriented Design + Parallel-rooted, Ordered Slip-stack Hierarchical planner

SSS Components in BOD

  • Start Minimally: only 4 primitives in the first version of the plan
  • Decompose Iteratively: agility for both designer and programmer as they can move freely between phases of the process
  • Minimise and Encapsulate: the most unique but easy to mess up by most novice BOD/POSH authors - how?
    - the logic of the tree is getting complex IF more than 3 sensors are needed to trigger a competence
    - flag the programmer to offload the logic from the tree onto a new sensor for simplification

Case Study 2: FAtiMA

  • FAtiMA: multi-agent architecture, each agent has an emotional state and plans future actions to achieve a specify goal
  • Goals can be weighted according to importance
  • How to perform authoring in FAtiMA?
    by editing several separate XML files
  • The process started by considering the motivations of the NPCs
    either corporative or uncooperative

Active Pursuit Goal of NPC

SSS Components in FAtiMA

  • Goals first: two teams are working on defining the goals
  • Find Decision Point: the decision making points are motivated by a goal
    - temporal ordering of decision by creating goal preconditions
  • Goal Weighting and Tuning: goal weights completely control how different agents act - what is consequence? -
    - the most time-consuming process to manipulate the personality files
  • Intent Goals for Future Consequences: not part of the scenario, but opens discussions for additional FAtiMA SSS
    - some claimed that FAtiMA cannot have more than one active pursuit goal at a time, but one team of this case study showed that it's possible.

Authoring Support Strategy

What did they do?
  • proposed an interface where authors can create example sequences of events schematically
  • prompt the user to create the corresponding goals when a denison is needed

Case Study 3: ABL

ABL

  • Designed to focus on the creation of expressive IVAs
  • Provides a feature-full reactive planning language
  • High degree of interactivity
  • Primary structure primitive in ABL is the behaviour
  • Active Behaviour Tree (ABT) for structure with potential executable steps as leaves
  • Working Memory Elements (WMEs) hold information to be shared throughout the ABT

ABL ABT

Example behaviour for a character to hand an object to another

SSS Components in ABL

  • ABL shared the same SSS components as BOD/POSH
    • Start Minimally: only 4 primitives in the first version of the plan
    • Decompose Iteratively: agility for both designer and programmer as they can move freely between phases of the process
  • Define coding idioms: ABL is unique to keep the complexity within the denison making process of the agent
  • Consider Interruption: the behaviour may have to multitask with other behaviours so need to carefully thinking of interrupting at some point.

Authoring Support Strategy

What did they find?
  • Discuss ABL SSS with authors of different levels of experiences
    ABL meta-behaviours feature was not utilised by some
  • ABL is hard to visualise
  • ABL debugger was not used for the usage barriers
  • What are they going to do?
    - create a graphical ABT representation for: parallel views of ABT, saving snapshots, and saving viewing locations

Conclusion

  • Proposed SSS requirement methodology
  • Carried out interviews with groups using different authoring processes
  • Proposed authoring strategies that may ease their authoring burdens
  • Potentially be applied to other IVA architectures as well.
